Spring Planting Guide

Spring is the busiest season for homesteaders. Plan your garden, start seeds indoors, and prepare for new life on the farm.

🗓️ Month-by-Month Breakdown

🌷 March — Early Spring

  • Start tomato, pepper, and eggplant seeds indoors under grow lights
  • Order seed potatoes and onion sets for early planting
  • Clean and organize garden tools; sharpen shovels and shears
  • Repair raised beds and replenish soil with compost
  • Begin pruning fruit trees and berry bushes before bud break
  • Order baby chicks from hatcheries for April delivery

🌼 April — Mid Spring

  • Direct-sow cool-season crops: peas, lettuce, spinach, carrots, beets, radishes
  • Plant seed potatoes and onion sets as soon as soil is workable
  • Brood baby chicks in a warm, safe brooder setup
  • Harden off cool-weather transplants like cabbage and kale
  • Set up rain barrels and clean gutters for April showers
  • Divide and transplant perennial herbs and flowers

🌸 May — Late Spring

  • Transplant tomatoes, peppers, eggplants, and squash after last frost
  • Plant warm-season crops: beans, corn, cucumbers, melons
  • Move young chicks to the coop and introduce to the flock
  • Mulch garden beds to retain moisture and suppress weeds
  • Set up soaker hoses or drip irrigation for summer watering
  • Monitor for frost and have row covers ready just in case

Summer Growing & Harvest

Keep the garden watered, harvest regularly, and preserve the bounty for winter.

🗓️ Month-by-Month Breakdown

☀️ June — Early Summer

  • Harvest cool-season crops before they bolt in the heat
  • Plant succession crops of beans, squash, and cucumbers
  • Set up trellises for vining crops like tomatoes and pole beans
  • Monitor for pests: aphids, tomato hornworms, squash bugs
  • Water deeply and consistently; mulch to reduce evaporation
  • Begin canning and freezing early strawberry and berry harvests

🌞 July — Mid Summer

  • Peak harvest: tomatoes, peppers, squash, cucumbers, beans, corn
  • Start fall crops: broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage from seed indoors
  • Water bath can tomato sauce, salsa, and pickles
  • Dehydrate herbs, tomatoes, and fruit for winter storage
  • Check beehives for honey stores and swarm potential
  • Provide extra water and shade for livestock during heat waves

🌻 August — Late Summer

  • Direct-sow fall greens: lettuce, spinach, kale, arugula
  • Transplant fall brassicas to the garden
  • Continue preserving: can salsa, freeze corn, dry beans
  • Start seeds for cool-weather crops to harvest in fall
  • Prune tomato plants to encourage late-season ripening
  • Plan and order garlic bulbs for fall planting

Fall Harvest & Preparation

Finish harvesting, prepare the garden for winter, and stock up on supplies.

🗓️ Month-by-Month Breakdown

🍂 September — Early Fall

  • Harvest remaining summer crops: tomatoes, peppers, eggplants
  • Continue harvesting fall greens and root vegetables
  • Plant garlic cloves 4-6 weeks before first expected frost
  • Start preserving apples: sauce, cider, butter, pie filling
  • Begin cleaning up garden beds; pull spent plants to compost
  • Stock up on chicken feed and animal supplies for winter

🍁 October — Mid Fall

  • Final harvest of pumpkins, squash, and root vegetables before frost
  • Cure pumpkins and winter squash in a warm, dry area
  • Plant spring-blooming bulbs and cover crops in empty beds
  • Can and preserve root vegetables, apples, and late tomatoes
  • Prepare chicken coop for winter: insulate, check heaters, stock bedding
  • Stack firewood and move it under cover for the heating season

🌰 November — Late Fall

  • Harvest cold-hardy greens: kale, collards, mache from under row covers
  • Empty and store garden hoses; drain sprinkler systems
  • Apply a thick layer of compost or mulch to perennial beds
  • Clean and store all garden tools with a light coat of oil
  • Bring tender herbs and container plants indoors for winter
  • Inventory preserved food and calculate remaining self-sufficiency

Winter Planning & Maintenance

Plan next year's garden, order seeds, and keep your animals warm.

🗓️ Month-by-Month Breakdown

❄️ December — Early Winter

  • Review this year's garden notes and plan crop rotation for next year
  • Order seed catalogs and start a wish list of new varieties
  • Check stored root vegetables and squash; remove any spoiling
  • Keep chicken waterers thawed; use heated bases if needed
  • Sharpen and oil all garden tools during downtime
  • Attend virtual homesteading classes or read new books

⛄ January — Mid Winter

  • Order seeds for next year's garden before popular varieties sell out
  • Start onion and leek seeds indoors under grow lights
  • Clean and organize seed inventory; test old seeds for germination
  • Plan new projects: build raised beds, design a new coop, plan orchards
  • Monitor solar battery levels and adjust usage on short days
  • Make meal plans from stored food to use older preserves first

🌨️ February — Late Winter

  • Start early brassica seeds: cabbage, broccoli, cauliflower indoors
  • Begin starting tomato and pepper seeds for early spring transplanting
  • Prune fruit trees and berry bushes on mild days
  • Order baby chicks and supplies for spring arrival
  • Map out garden beds and finalize planting schedule
  • Clean and disinfect seed trays, pots, and greenhouse surfaces
