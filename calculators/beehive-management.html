How It Works

This calculator is based on guidelines from the American Beekeeping Federation and university extension programs. Honey production varies significantly based on hive type, location, and forage availability.

Honey Production Factors

  • Hive Type: Langstroth hives typically produce 60–100 lbs/hive, while top-bar hives produce 20–40 lbs/hive
  • Climate: Longer growing seasons mean more nectar flow
  • Nectar Flow: Strong forage (clover, wildflowers) = higher yields
Annual Honey = Hives × Base Production × Nectar Flow Factor × Climate Factor Winter Feed = Hives × 60 lbs (minimum for cold climates) Swarm Risk = (Colony Strength ÷ Available Space) × 100
