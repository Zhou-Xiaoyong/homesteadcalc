Seasonal Planting Calendar

Early Spring: Cool-season crops — lettuce, spinach, peas, carrots, beets

Mid Spring: Broccoli, cabbage, kale, potatoes

Late Spring: Warm-season crops after last frost — tomatoes, peppers, squash, beans, corn

Early Summer: Continue warm-season planting — okra, sweet potatoes, eggplant

Mid Summer: Second planting of beans, cucumbers, squash

Late Summer: Start fall crops — lettuce, spinach, carrots for fall harvest

Early Fall: Harvest summer crops, plant cover crops

Mid Fall: Plant garlic, shallots for next year

Late Fall: Prepare garden for winter — mulch beds, compost

Winter: Plan next year's garden, order seeds, start indoor seedlings (depending on zone)

Indoor Starting: Start tomatoes, peppers 6–8 weeks before last frost

How It Works

This tool uses data from the USDA Plant Hardiness Zone Map. The map divides North America into 13 zones based on the average annual minimum winter temperature.

Zone Information

  • Zones 1–3: Coldest zones, short growing seasons (80–110 days)
  • Zones 4–6: Cool temperate, moderate growing seasons (120–180 days)
  • Zones 7–9: Warm temperate, long growing seasons (180–250 days)
  • Zones 10–13: Subtropical/tropical, year-round growing
Pro Tip: Use your zone to select plants that will thrive in your climate. Each zone represents a 10°F temperature difference in minimum winter temperatures.
