How It Works

Our cider making calculator uses standard home cider making ratios based on apple type, cider style, and target alcohol content. These values are derived from established cidermaking references and extension service guidelines widely used by home cider makers and small-scale cidermakers.

Juice Yield by Apple Type

Apples are roughly 84% water, but actual juice yield depends on variety and pressing method. A good rule of thumb is that 1 bushel (42 lbs) of apples yields about 3–4 gallons of juice. Heirloom cider apples often have higher juice content and better sugar/acid balance than standard dessert apples.

Cider Making Formula

Juice Yield (gal) = Apple Weight (lbs) × 0.08–0.10 Sugar for ABV ≈ (Target ABV ÷ 0.55) × Juice Volume × 0.104 Yeast ≈ 0.5–1 gram per gallon of juice Bottles (750ml) = Total Liters ÷ 0.75

Cider Type Differences

Hard cider undergoes full alcoholic fermentation, converting most sugar to alcohol. Sweet cider is fresh pressed apple juice that is either pasteurized or kept refrigerated to prevent fermentation. Sparkling cider can be either alcoholic (carbonated hard cider) or non-alcoholic (carbonated apple juice), with carbonation added through secondary fermentation or forced carbonation.

Pro Tip: For the best cider, use a blend of sweet, tart, and bittersharp apples. Aim for roughly 40% sweet, 40% tart, and 20% bittersharp/heirloom varieties. Always test your juice with a hydrometer before fermentation begins to get an accurate starting gravity reading.

Actual juice yield varies based on apple ripeness, pressing method, and equipment. Commercial orchards with hydraulic presses can get 4–5 gallons per bushel.

Did You Know?

It takes approximately 36 apples to make one gallon of apple cider. One standard apple tree can produce enough apples for about 15–20 gallons of cider in a good year. That's roughly 500–700 apples per tree!
