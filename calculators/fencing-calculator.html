How It Works

This calculator determines fencing materials based on standard spacing for each fence type. Posts are calculated differently depending on fence style:

Fence Type → Post Spacing: Woven wire field fence : posts every 8 ft High-tensile electric : posts every 20–30 ft (we use 25 ft) Barbed wire : posts every 12 ft Split rail : posts every 8 ft Picket / Privacy : posts every 6–8 ft (we use 7 ft) Chain link : posts every 8–10 ft (we use 9 ft) Cattle panel : 16 ft panels → ceiling(feet / 16) Hog panel : 16 ft panels → ceiling(feet / 16) Poultry netting : posts every 8 ft (support posts) Corner posts: 2 per corner (H-brace assembly) + 2 additional corner posts per gate location Wire quantity: Woven wire = linear ft × 1.05 (5% waste factor) Barbed wire = linear ft × strands × 1.10 (10% waste) High-tensile = linear ft × strands × 1.05 (5% waste) Poultry netting = linear ft × 1.05 (5% waste) Staples / clips: line posts × 4 per post × strands

Choosing the Right Fence for Your Livestock

🐔 Chickens & Poultry

Best: Poultry netting (1/2" or 1" mesh), welded wire. Electric netting for rotational grazing.

Height: 4–6 ft to deter predators climbing.

🐑 Sheep & Goats

Best: Woven wire (goats need 4" or smaller mesh), high-tensile electric (5-strand for goats).

Height: 4–5 ft. Goats climb — add electric top strand.

🐄 Cattle & Horses

Best: Barbed wire (3–5 strand), high-tensile electric, woven wire with top board for horses.

Height: 4–5 ft. Horses need visibility — use white ribbon or boards.

🐖 Pigs

Best: Hog panels (not climbers!), electric wire (they respect it after one shock).

Height: 3–4 ft. Pigs root under — bury mesh 6" or use electric apron.

🦌 Deer Protection

Best: Woven wire 8 ft tall, high-tensile 8–10 ft, or electric 8–10 ft.

Height: Minimum 8 ft. Deer jump high — go tall or use double fence.

🏡 Garden & Property

Best: Woven wire, polypropylene deer fence, or picket (decorative).

Height: 4–6 ft for garden, 3 ft for decorative picket.

Fence Planning Tips

  • Always buy 10% extra materials for mistakes and gates
  • Use H-braces at every corner and gate
  • Bury wire mesh 6" to stop digging predators
  • Mark property lines before buying materials
  • Check local zoning for fence height limits

Cost-Saving Ideas

  • Use T-posts between wood corner braces
  • Buy wire in full rolls (less waste)
  • Consider used livestock panels from neighbors
  • High-tensile is cheapest per foot for large areas
