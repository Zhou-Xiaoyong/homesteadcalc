    How It Works

    This self-sufficiency assessment is based on 8 key areas of homestead independence. Each area has multiple questions scored from 1 to 5.

    Scoring Methodology: - 38 questions across 8 categories - Each question scored 1–5 - Maximum possible score: 190 - Categories weighted equally (23.75 points max each) Level Rankings: Novice (0–38) : Just starting out Beginner (39–76) : Some homesteading activities Growing (77–114) : Building serious self-sufficiency Advanced (115–152) : Significant independence Near Self-Sufficient (153–180) : Very high independence Master Homesteader (181–190) : Near complete self-reliance The radar chart shows each category's percentage score relative to its maximum (23.75 points per category). This assessment is meant as a self-reflection tool, not a scientific measurement. Your goal should be continuous improvement in areas most important to your situation.

    Why These 8 Categories Matter

    True self-sufficiency isn't just about growing food. Water, energy, shelter, health, skills, community, and financial resilience are all essential. A homestead with a great garden but no water independence is only partially self-sufficient. This assessment helps you identify blind spots.

    Self-Sufficiency Tips

    • Start with 1–2 categories; don't try everything at once
    • Food is the easiest category to improve quickly
    • Financial resilience underlies everything
    • Community reduces infrastructure needs

    Score Improvement

    Most homesteaders score 80–140. A score above 160 is exceptional. Retake this assessment every 6–12 months to track your progress.
