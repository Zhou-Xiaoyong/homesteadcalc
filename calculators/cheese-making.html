How It Works

Our cheese making calculator uses traditional cheese yield formulas and ingredient ratios based on milk type, fat content, and cheese variety. The calculations follow established cheesemaking principles from university extension programs and master cheesemakers.

Cheese Yield Explained

Cheese yield depends primarily on the protein (casein) and fat content of the milk. On average, cow's milk produces about 10–15% cheese by weight — meaning 10 pounds of milk yields roughly 1–1.5 pounds of cheese. Sheep's milk, with its higher protein and fat content, can produce 15–20% yield. Fresh cheeses like ricotta and mozzarella have different formulas since ricotta is made from whey and mozzarella is stretched curd.

Formula

Cheese Yield (lbs) = Milk Weight × Yield Percentage × Fat Factor Rennet = Milk Volume × Rennet per Gallon Salt = Cheese Yield × Salt Percentage Milk Weight = Volume × 8.6 lbs/gal (cow), 8.4 lbs/gal (goat), 9.1 lbs/gal (sheep) Total Yield = (Casein + Fat) × Recovery Rate
Pro Tip: For the best cheese yield, use fresh, high-quality milk that hasn't been ultra-pasteurized (UHT). Raw or pasteurized (not ultra-pasteurized) milk works best. Goat's milk produces softer cheese with a tangy flavor, while sheep's milk is richer and produces higher yields. Always measure pH during cheesemaking — it's the most reliable indicator of proper curd development.

A typical 2-gallon batch makes about 1.5–2.5 lbs of hard cheese, or roughly one small wheel. The whey byproduct can be used for ricotta or animal feed.

Did You Know?

It takes about 10 pounds (roughly 1.2 gallons) of milk to make 1 pound of hard cheese. About 90% of the milk's weight becomes whey — the watery byproduct that's rich in protein and can be used to make ricotta.
