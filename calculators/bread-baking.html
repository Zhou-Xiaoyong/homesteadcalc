How It Works

Our bread baking calculator uses baker's percentages — a professional baking technique where every ingredient is expressed as a percentage of the total flour weight. This method allows bakers to easily scale recipes up or down while maintaining perfect consistency.

Baker's Math Basics

In baker's percentages, flour is always 100%. All other ingredients are calculated relative to the flour weight. For example, a 70% hydration dough means the water weighs 70% of the flour weight. This system is used worldwide in professional bakeries because it makes scaling and comparing recipes straightforward.

Formula

Total Dough Weight = Number of Loaves × Loaf Size Flour (g) = Total Dough Weight ÷ (1 + Hydration % + Salt % + Yeast %) Water (g) = Flour × Hydration % Salt (g) = Flour × Salt % Yeast / Starter (g) = Flour × Yeast %
Pro Tip: Fermentation times vary significantly with temperature. The times provided assume a room temperature of 70–75°F (21–24°C). For every 5°F warmer, reduce fermentation time by about 25%. For cooler temperatures, increase time accordingly.

Quick Reference

Baker's Percentages by Bread Type

Bread Hydration Salt
Sourdough 65–75% 2%
Sandwich 60–65% 1.8%
Baguette 65–70% 1.8%
Pizza 60–70% 2%
Focaccia 75–80% 2%
Ciabatta 75–80% 2%

Commercial yeast is typically 1–2% of flour weight. Sourdough starter ranges from 10–30% depending on desired flavor and fermentation speed.

Did You Know?

A single gram of active dry yeast contains roughly 20 billion yeast cells. During fermentation, these cells feed on sugars in the dough, producing carbon dioxide gas that leavens the bread and alcohol that contributes to the bread's complex flavor.
