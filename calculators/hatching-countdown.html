How It Works

Our hatching countdown timer uses standard incubation periods for each bird species, based on data from poultry science extensions and experienced breeders. Incubation stages are divided into four key phases: setting, turning, lockdown, and hatching.

Incubation Stages Explained

Understanding the stages of incubation helps you provide the right care at the right time. Each stage has specific temperature, humidity, and turning requirements that are critical for successful hatching.

Incubation Periods by Bird Type

Chicken: 21 days Duck: 28 days Turkey: 28 days Goose: 30 days Quail: 18 days Pheasant: 23 days
Pro Tip: Always mark your calendar with both the set date and expected hatch date. Eggs can hatch up to 24–48 hours early or late depending on temperature fluctuations and individual egg variation.

Did You Know?

During the final 3 days of incubation (lockdown), chicks position themselves for hatching by pipping the air cell. Stop turning eggs during lockdown and increase humidity to 65–75% to prevent the membrane from drying out.
