How It Works

Our mushroom growing calculator uses established biological efficiency (BE) values and growth timelines based on mushroom species and cultivation method. Biological efficiency measures how efficiently a mushroom strain converts substrate dry weight into mushroom fresh weight, expressed as a percentage.

Biological Efficiency Explained

Biological Efficiency (BE) is the standard metric in mushroom cultivation, calculated as the ratio of fresh mushroom weight to dry substrate weight, expressed as a percentage. For example, a 100% BE means 1 pound of dry substrate produces 1 pound of fresh mushrooms. Different species naturally have different BE potentials — oyster mushrooms can achieve 100–150% BE, while shiitake on logs typically ranges from 30–50%.

Formula

Expected Yield (lbs) = Substrate Weight × Biological Efficiency (%) × Experience Factor Total Timeline = Colonization Time + Fruiting Time Estimated Value = Expected Yield × Price per Pound Total Harvest = Yield per Flush × Number of Flushes
Pro Tip: Maintaining proper temperature, humidity, and fresh air exchange (FAE) is critical for achieving optimal biological efficiency. Beginners typically achieve 60–80% of the maximum BE, while advanced growers can reach 90–110%+ of expected yields with optimized conditions.

Did You Know?

Mushrooms can double in size every 24 hours during peak fruiting. A tiny pinhead can become a full-sized oyster mushroom in just 3–5 days under optimal conditions.
