How It Works

Our hay calculator uses standardized dry matter intake rates based on livestock type and body weight. These values are derived from university extension research and are widely used by livestock producers across the US.

Dry Matter Intake Basics

Livestock consume approximately 2–3% of their body weight in dry matter daily. As-fed hay (which contains 10–15% moisture) translates to roughly 2.2–3.5% of body weight per day. Dairy cows and growing animals have higher requirements, while animals on pasture or grain supplements need less hay.

Formula

Daily Hay (lbs) = Number of Animals × Weight (lbs) × Intake Rate × Quality Factor × Feed Type Factor Weekly Bales = (Daily Hay × 7) ÷ Bale Weight Monthly Tons = (Daily Hay × 30) ÷ 2000 Monthly Cost = (Daily Hay × 30 ÷ Bale Weight) × Price per Bale
Pro Tip: Always buy 10–15% more hay than you calculate for waste, weather losses, and unexpected feeding needs. Hay stored outside can lose 20–30% of its value to weather damage.

A 1000 lb horse eats about 20–25 lbs of hay per day — roughly half of a 50 lb bale. Plan accordingly for winter feeding when pasture is unavailable.

Did You Know?

A ton of hay is 2000 pounds — about 40 small square bales at 50 lbs each. A single 1000 lb cow needs roughly 1.5 tons of hay for a 6-month winter feeding period.
