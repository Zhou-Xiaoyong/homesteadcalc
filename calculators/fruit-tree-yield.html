How It Works

This calculator uses yield data from USDA Extension Service and university horticulture programs. Yields vary based on tree type, rootstock, age, and growing conditions.

Yield Factors

  • Age: Young trees (1–3 years) produce minimal fruit; peak production at 5–15 years
  • Rootstock: Dwarf trees produce 5–15 lbs/tree; standard trees 50–200 lbs/tree
  • Conditions: Full sun (6+ hours) and well-drained soil maximize yields
Per Tree Yield = Base Yield × Age Factor × Rootstock Factor × Condition Factor Total Yield = Per Tree Yield × Number of Trees Bushels = Total Yield ÷ Weight per Bushel Jars = Total Yield × 1.5 (average yield per pint jar)
