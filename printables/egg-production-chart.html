4. What Affects Laying

☀️ Daylight

Hens need 14–16 hours of light for peak lay. Production drops sharply below 12 hours. A coop light on a timer (on before dawn) sustains winter laying.

🍂 Molting

Annual molt (usually fall, 8–16 weeks) pauses laying while feathers regrow. Boost protein to 18–20% during molt to shorten it.

🌡️ Temperature

Ideal laying range is 50–75°F. Heat above 90°F and deep cold both reduce output. Shade + cool water in summer; draft-free coop in winter.

🥚 Nutrition & Stress

16–18% protein layer feed with 3–4% calcium. Predator scares, new flock members, or moving the coop can pause laying for days to weeks.
