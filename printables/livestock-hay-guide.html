4. Hay Quality & Storage

🌿 Judging Quality

Green color, sweet smell, fine stems, leafy texture. Reject musty, dusty, brown, or weed-heavy bales. Second cutting is usually softer and richer than first.

🐐 Match Hay to Animal

Grass hay (timothy, orchard, brome) suits most animals. Alfalfa (high protein & calcium) is for milkers and growing stock — too rich as sole feed for dry does or geldings.

🏠 Storage Rules

Off the ground on pallets, under cover, with airflow. Properly stored hay keeps 1–2 years with modest nutrient loss; ground-stored round bales can lose a third.

🔥 Fire Safety

Never barn-stack hay above 20% moisture — fermenting wet hay self-heats and can ignite. New bales: monitor for heat the first 6 weeks.
